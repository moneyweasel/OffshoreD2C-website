The product

Not a fund. Not an adviser recommendation. A listed instrument you buy yourself.

The NG Tactical Growth ETP sits on the London Stock Exchange exactly like a share. You buy it at market price through any broker that supports LSE-listed securities. No minimum holding period. No lock-in. Settlement in the standard T+2 cycle.

The strategy inside it is different. It uses the Hedgeye Quad macro framework — a rules-based system that maps the rate of change in economic growth and inflation to determine where capital should be allocated at any point in the cycle.

When conditions favour growth assets, the portfolio holds them. When they deteriorate, exposure is reduced. That asymmetry — protecting the downside before chasing the upside — is the long-term performance driver.

The method

Rules, not gut calls.

Most portfolios are built around what a manager believes will happen. This one is built around what a framework says is happening — and has been happening, consistently, for decades.

01

Macro Regime Framework

The Hedgeye Quad model divides the economic cycle into four quadrants based on the rate of change in GDP growth and CPI inflation. Each quadrant historically favours a different asset class profile. The model is updated regularly with current data.

02

Rules-Based Allocation

Allocation is determined by the current Quad reading — not by conviction, client sentiment or market noise. The same rules apply in every cycle. When the data changes, the allocation changes. No discretion. No emotion. No narrative bias.

03

Asymmetric Compounding

A 50% drawdown requires a 100% recovery. Protecting capital in downturns matters more than maximising peak returns. By managing the severity and duration of losses first, the long-term compounding rate improves — even with lower peak exposure during bull markets.

The strategy is embedded in an LSE-listed ETP structure. The underlying allocation is rebalanced in line with macro regime transitions.
