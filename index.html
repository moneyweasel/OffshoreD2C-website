The system inside it is built on institutional research and hedge fund methodology — an economic regime framework that identifies four distinct market environments and systematically rotates asset allocation to match. It uses verified global data from institutions including the Bank for International Settlements, the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund.

The system

Four economic environments. One adaptive portfolio.

Markets are not random. They respond to economic conditions — specifically to whether growth and inflation are accelerating or decelerating. The NG system is built around that principle.

Expansion

Growth ↑ · Inflation stable

Conditions favour growth-oriented assets. The portfolio tilts toward global equities and risk assets to capture the expansion in economic activity.

Inflationary

Growth ↑ · Inflation ↑

Growth continues but inflation is elevated. The allocation shifts toward assets with pricing resilience — commodities, energy, inflation-linked instruments.

Contractionary

Growth ↓ · Inflation falling

Economic activity is weakening. Capital preservation takes priority. The portfolio moves toward government bonds and defensive asset classes.

Stagflationary

Growth ↓ · Inflation ↑

The most challenging environment. Slow growth combined with rising prices. The system positions defensively, reducing exposure to both equities and nominal bonds.

01

GDP & CPI Rate of Change Matrix

The framework tracks the rate of change — not absolute levels — in growth and inflation data from the BIS, World Bank and IMF. Direction of travel determines regime, not the headline number.

02

Proprietary Trend Signal

A systematic price trend signal must confirm any regime reading before allocation changes. This prevents premature repositioning on ambiguous or transitional data.

03

Policy Overlay

Central bank policy stance is assessed as a final confirmation layer. Regime, trend and policy must be in agreement before the portfolio rebalances. No single signal acts alone.

The strategy is embedded in an LSE-listed ETP structure issued by Leverage Shares. The underlying allocation rebalances in line with confirmed regime transitions.
